I improved productivity and efficiency by taking regular breaks during the project that helped to stay focused. I was also being realistic by doing the project bit by bit to ensure that I was overwhelmed. Basically I would 2 questions at a time thus I divided the project into small achievable tasks. If I was part of a team I guess the best way would be effective communication through feedback so that everyone in the team is aware of the progress of the project and the goals. Training is another way to improve efficiency and productivity. competent employees don’t waste time trying to figure out what to do but they get on with the task at hand. Incentives can be a motivator to improve productivity, for instance lets say if we finish a certain task by Friday in the morning then we can finish work at 3pm instead of 5pm and get paid for a full day. Trust me I will make sure that I have finished my targets by Friday morning and 3pm on the dot I am ready to go its Friday after all. Team work is also important, sharing of ideas, division of tasks and all the benefits that comes with team work. Trello is a good example for a team to use to plan and organise, if used appropriately it can help to improve efficiency and productivity.
